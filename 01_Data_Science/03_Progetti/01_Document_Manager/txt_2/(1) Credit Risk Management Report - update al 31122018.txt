Executive Summary – key points as at the 31st of December 2018
 In December End of Year values have substantially confirmed the dynamics highlighted in previous months.
 <U+F071> About the performing portfolio End of Year (EoY) values highlight the following:
        <U+2713> lending quality on strengthening flow in terms of probability of default has been confirmed (actual average PD at 1,12% vs. a target of 1,64%);
        <U+2713> in December new lending volume’s growth slowed down sharply, so that actual EoY strenghtening flow is lower than expected value (actual €9,2 bn vs. €10,3 bn expected);
        <U+2713> it’s worth highlighting, therefore, the significant decrease of EAD and RWAs of AIRB performing portfolio observed in December (-€2019 mln and -€1.387 respectively),
           driven by Corporate Division (-€1.646 mln and -€1.219 respectively);
        <U+2713> actual EoY default flow’s volume reached €1.727 bn, €78 mln higher than expected, due to the default flow observed in December (€271 mln), while EoY default flow’s cost
           reached €557 mln, €115 mln higher than budget;
        <U+2713> in December, the decrease of average LGD of the AIRB performing portfolio observed in November continued (25,57% , -45 bps vs. November, -57 bps vs. December 2017):
           to highlight the decrease (-27 bps) of Corporate Division (mainly due to the decrease of unsecured loans by €1499 mln) and the decrease of Retail Division by 10 bps, mainly
           driven, also in this case, by a lower weight of unsecured loans.
 <U+F071> On the other side, the highlights concerning the non-performing portfolio:
        <U+2713> EoY Utop cure rate (restructured and “rischio anomalo” loans) remained lower than the target (6,85% actual vs. 8,5% expected);
        <U+2713> EoY bad loans portfolio recovery rate reached 5,2%, far lower the expected value (10,2%), affected by a delay of the Juliet’s start;
        <U+2713> in December the exposures related to the disposals of Merlino (€1.993 mln) and Alpha2 (€465 mln) have been derecognized.
 <U+F071> The cost of credit as of the 31st of December 2018 is equal to €653 mln, €111 mln lower than budget value of €764 mln (€255,5 mln the gap vs. budget in November); the
    performing portfolio’s cost reached €388 mln, substantially in line with the budget (-€ 96,9 mln the gap vs. budget in November), while the cost of non-performing portfolio is equal
    to €265 mln, €115 lower than the expected value of 380 mln (-€ 158,6 mln the gap vs. budget in November).
