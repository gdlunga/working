Executive Summary – Highlights as at 31-03-2019
 STRATEGIC KRIs
 <U+25AA> Signals observed in February are confirmed.
 Adequacy: ICAAP & ILAAP
 <U+25AA> Capital Ratios, while slightly lower than in February, remain above the expected March values. As before, the Total Capital Ratio is below the yearly Tolerance target that is calibrated
    according to the planned T2 issues in the 2H2019.
 <U+25AA> Gross asset encumbrance ratio improves in March, thanks to a reduction in secured funding; it still breaches the annual Risk Capacity but is also better than the expected March
    Tolerance level. LCR , NSFR and the Gap Ratio 3y all increase respect to February.
 RWA
 <U+25AA> Regulatory RWA (P1) increase in March resulting over than the yearly target (Risk Appetite target for FY2019).
    The monthly dynamics highlights the negative effects stemming from the introduction of IFRS 16 and from the provisions for the customer reporting activity for diamond transactions
    and for disputes relating to the burden sharing event.
 <U+25AA> Increase in Market Risk RWA (P2) due to the rolling of the time window with increase of average VaR.
 <U+25AA> IRRBB Risk: The higher RWA (P2) level reflects a further increase in the DeltaEVE metric.
 Risk Limits
 <U+25AA> IRRBB: Widiba has increased the IRRBB Risk Limit breach (actual 9,1%, 5,9% in February vs 2,5% of RL). The Head Office authorized the overcoming of the limit until the end of June.
 <U+25AA> Liquidity Risk: Still present some breaches in the Gap Ratio profile. The NSFR in on the risk limit. The Gap Ratio 5y for MPSCS is breached.
