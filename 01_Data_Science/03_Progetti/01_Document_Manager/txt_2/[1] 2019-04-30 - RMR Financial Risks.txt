Executive Summary – Highlights as at 30-04-2019
Adequacy: ICAAP & ILAAP
<U+25AA> Capital Ratios: the CET1 Ratio is above the FY Appetite (+11 bps), as well as the period expected profile (+20 bps). The Total Capital Ratio current profile stands below both the FY
   Appetite and the period one (-17 bps) suggesting that its dynamics is to be closely monitored in the light of the planned T2 issue.
<U+25AA> Liquidity Ratios: the LCR is well above the Risk Appetite. The Structural Liquidity (NSFR, AE, Gap Ratios) remains weak. Potential difficulties on T2 issue could also affect the
   planned senior unsecured issues planned in the 4Q19.
RWA
<U+25AA> Regulatory RWA (P1): reduction w.r.t March (-627 €/mln), due mainly to the credit risk AIRB (which benefits of a reduction in the RWA density) and market risk trading book
   components (due to a lower IR sensitivity stemming from bonds and derivatives transactions).
<U+25AA> Internal RWA (P2): slight reduction w.r.t. to the previous month (-417 €/mln), as a net result of an increase in the market risk BB P2 (further effect of the rolling of the Var time window
   which has not yet incorporated all the severe increase of the Italian Spread from April/May 2018) and a decrease in IRRBB (following actions implemented by the Finance
   department).
<U+25AA> Both Regulatory and Internal RWA are already at the 2019 FY Appetite, also at the BU/LE level; the CEO (IFRS16 effect) and WDIBA (IRRBB effect) are beyond the yearly Appetite
   targets.
Risk Limits
<U+25AA> IRRBB: Widiba has increased the IRRBB Risk Limit breach (actual 12,8%, 9,1% in March vs 2,5% of RL). The Breach has been authorized by CEO until 30/06/2019, waiting for the
   activation of the procedure for trading in hedging derivatives with the Parent Company.
<U+25AA> Liquidity Risk: the NSFR is below the Risk Limit (110,7% vs. 111% RL), and AE Ratios and Gap 2Y/3Y remain breached. Actions are under evaluation with the relevant Business
   units.
